## The headline insight: a regime change inside the last 2 months

| Signal | n=243 (2 wks) IC | n=1363 (2 mos) IC | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| `rsi_14` | **−0.476** | **+0.181** | **Sign flip** |
| `ret_21d` | **−0.491** | **+0.107** | **Sign flip** |
| `px_vs_sma20_pct` | **−0.318** | **+0.151** | **Sign flip** |
| `brent` | −0.149 | **+0.420** | Strong macro driver over 2 months only |
| `gold` | −0.026 | **−0.366** | Strong inverse driver over 2 months only |
| `usdpkr` | +0.137 | −0.095 | Mostly noise |
| `news_score` | −0.564 (n=15) | −0.028 (n=46) | Discount: small samples |

### What this means in plain English

For roughly **8 of the last 10 weeks** the PSX has been in a **normal
momentum regime**: stocks with high RSI, high 21-day momentum, and
prices above their 20-day SMA outperformed by ~+1.5% over the next 5
days. Bottom-tercile names underperformed by −1% to −1.7%. This is
textbook trend-following behaviour, and the bot's existing
long-side bias matches it.

But the **last 2 weeks have inverted**. RSI IC went from +0.18 to
−0.48 — a 0.66-point swing. Top-tercile (overbought) names now
*lose* −1.9% over 5 days while bottom-tercile (oversold) names
*gain* +4.85%. In other words, the very signals that worked over the
2-month average have been actively hurting the long side over the
last fortnight.

This is a classic late-cycle pattern: a long uptrend tops out,
overbought names mean-revert hard, oversold deep-value names
suddenly outperform. It is a regime change, not a permanent
condition.

### Two distinct lessons

1. **The Short Ideas tab is correctly tuned for the *current* regime.**
   It rewards overbought + below-SMA + falling-21d names — exactly
   the names that have lost money in the last 2 weeks. Sell calls
   show 93.75% hit rate.

2. **The long-side picker is correctly tuned for the *2-month* regime.**
   Over the broader window, momentum signals work. The bot's
   bullish picks failed in the last 2 weeks specifically because
   the regime flipped — they would have worked beautifully over
   the 2-month average.

The fix is therefore **not** to invert the long-side strategy — that
would lose money 80% of the time. The fix is a **regime detector**
that down-weights momentum-style long picks when the most recent 10
trading days show negative price-signal IC.

## Macro driver findings (60-day window only — 14-day is too noisy)

Two macro signals show large, statistically meaningful predictive
power across the 35-stock universe:

- **Brent crude IC +0.42.** When Brent rises, the cross-section of
  PSX stocks rises 5 days later. Buy-side hit rate at top-tercile
  Brent = 65%, sell-side hit rate at bottom-tercile = 74%. Effect
  size is enormous (top-tercile mean fwd +2.9%, bottom-tercile
  −3.3%). This is consistent with KSE-100 being heavily E&P /
  refinery / OMC weighted (~24% of the index by sector).

- **Gold IC −0.37.** When gold rises (risk-off globally), PSX
  underperforms. Top-tercile gold = bullish gold = 27.7% buy hit
  rate (mean fwd −3.2%); bottom-tercile gold = +3.1%. This is the
  classic global risk-on / risk-off relationship.

These two drivers should be elevated inside the macro-impact engine
— right now they are weighted equally with smaller drivers like
copper or cotton, which had near-zero IC.

USD/PKR shows negligible cross-sectional IC (−0.10 over 60 days,
+0.14 over 14 days). Rupee moves are universe-wide and don't
discriminate between names; they reach the score correctly through
the sector verdicts (export-heavy sectors like textiles, IT benefit
when the rupee weakens) rather than as a stand-alone bullish signal.

## Phase-2 priorities (re-confirmed by the 2-month run)

1. **Regime detector.** A small rolling-IC monitor that classifies
   the current regime as MOMENTUM / MEAN-REVERSION / MIXED based
   on the last 10–15 trading days of price-signal IC. The bot
   should down-weight long-side momentum picks (or even invert
   them) when the regime is MEAN-REVERSION.

2. **Brent and gold elevated in macro-impact engine.** Both have
   IC magnitudes >0.35 over 60 days; they deserve more weight than
   smaller commodities.

3. **HIGH conviction recalibration.** HIGH-conv calls have HIGHER
   MAE than MEDIUM. Either tighten the criteria to issue a HIGH
   call, or downgrade the prior on HIGH conviction so it requires
   stronger multi-source agreement.

4. **Date-version fundamentals + management tone.** Engine 1
   currently can't backtest these because the parquets are
   latest-only. Phase 2 needs `data/fundamentals_history.parquet`
   with timestamps so the synthesizer's individual lenses can be
   IC-tested.

5. **Per-name strategy review for the 0% bucket.** `MCB`, `PPL`,
   `PSO`, `NPL` — the bot has been wrong on every prediction. Use
   a sector-aware override or freeze predictions on these names
   until the underlying model is repaired.
